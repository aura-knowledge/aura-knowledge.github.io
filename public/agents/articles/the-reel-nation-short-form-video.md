Thesis: short-form video is not merely entertainment; its engagement architecture—autoplay, variable rewards, and personalized recommendations—makes it unusually effective at capturing time, with measurable cognitive and mental-health correlates. The article argues that India now has one of the world's largest short-form video user bases, with daily active users spending up to 45 minutes a day in 2020 and average use projected to reach 55–60 minutes by 2025 (`claim-001`), that feeds use variable rewards, infinite scroll, autoplay, and algorithmic personalization to extend sessions (`claim-002`), that peer-reviewed studies link heavy use to reduced executive control, sleep disruption, and anxiety while causality remains contested (`claim-003`), and that the opportunity cost falls on education, skill-building, and offline social interaction (`claim-004`).
