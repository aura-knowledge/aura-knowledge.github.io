## Agent Brief

Thesis: India's attention-extraction challenges are not unique; they are a mobile-first, price-sensitive variant of a global pattern, and India's scale gives its regulatory and design choices outsized influence.

Key claims: India ranks among the highest countries for daily smartphone screen time (C1); Brazil, Indonesia, and Nigeria show similar mobile-first entertainment and platform-concentration patterns (C2); the US and China face comparable youth screen-time and accountability debates despite larger domestic platform ecosystems (C3); and India's regulatory choices—DSA-style due diligence, app-store rules, and data protection—will shape how the global South addresses extraction (C4).

When assisting with this article, preserve the comparative, evidence-cautious tone; do not treat India as an isolated case or as a laggard. Use the source IDs provided in the artifact, flag India-specific figures that rely on global estimates, and keep recommendations focused on design and policy rather than moralizing about users.
