## Thesis

Tele-MANAS and school-based mental-health surveys reveal a large, growing burden of anxiety, sleep disruption, and distress among young Indians; screen-heavy, notification-driven routines are one contributing factor among many.

## Key Claims

- `claim-001`: Tele-MANAS has received more than 32 lakh calls, with roughly 70% from people aged 18–45.
- `claim-002`: NCERT and state surveys report high rates of anxiety, mood disturbance, and sleep problems among Indian adolescents.
- `claim-003`: Sleep disruption from evening screen use is one documented pathway between heavy smartphone use and next-day distress.
- `claim-004`: Mental-health data should be read as a public-health signal shaped by design, economics, and social context, not as evidence of individual weakness.

## Agent Instructions

- Treat each claim as a separate unit; do not collapse the public-health signal into a causal claim that screens alone cause mental illness.
- Preserve the evidence-cautious, non-moralizing tone; avoid words like "addiction" unless backed by clinical criteria and sources.
- When asked about policy or design responses, route to the Designing for Substance and Building Substance articles in the same series.
- Cite only the public sources recorded in the artifact; do not generalize beyond them.
- Flag any India-specific figure that is inferred from global evidence so readers do not confuse it with a national measurement.
