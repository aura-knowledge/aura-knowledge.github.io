## Thesis

The Jio-led collapse in mobile-data prices made India one of the world's largest and cheapest internet markets, but the behavioral dividend has gone more toward consumption than creation or learning. This article treats the 2016 Jio launch as a historical hinge: it solved India's access crisis at extraordinary speed, then revealed that access alone does not determine whether a society uses a tool or is used by it.

## Claims

- `claim-001`: Jio's 2016 launch reduced Indian mobile-data prices to among the lowest in the world.
- `claim-002`: India added hundreds of millions of internet users in the following years, many in rural areas and on low-cost smartphones.
- `claim-003`: The same low-cost data enabled a surge in short-form video, gaming, and messaging.
- `claim-004`: The access success now raises a use question: can the same infrastructure be redirected toward productivity and learning?
