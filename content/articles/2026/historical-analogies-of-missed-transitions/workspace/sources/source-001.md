## Summary

The Maddison Project Database provides long-run estimates of GDP per capita and population for countries and regions from the early modern period onward. It is the standard source for comparing income trajectories across centuries and for tracking how early or late industrialization shaped relative living standards.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-001: Supports the analogy that early industrializers opened a persistent income lead over late adopters. The exact size and timing of the gap vary by country, but the directional pattern is well documented.

## Key snippet

> The Maddison Project Database is an extension of the original Maddison dataset, providing historical data on economic growth and income levels across countries and regions.

## Notes

Use this source descriptively, not as a precise causal claim. The database shows correlation between early adoption and long-run income, but many institutional and geopolitical factors also explain the gaps.
