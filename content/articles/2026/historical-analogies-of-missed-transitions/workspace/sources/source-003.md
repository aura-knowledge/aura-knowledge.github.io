## Summary

This IMF staff discussion note analyzes how generative AI could affect labor markets and productivity across countries. It finds that advanced economies are more exposed to AI automation but also better positioned to capture productivity gains, while emerging markets may see slower adoption and smaller near-term benefits.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-003: Supports the argument that AI could compound productivity gaps between leaders and laggards depending on adaptation, infrastructure, and workforce composition.

## Key snippet

> Advanced economies and some emerging markets are better positioned to capture near-term productivity gains from AI because of their higher shares of cognitive-task employment and stronger digital infrastructure.

## Notes

Use as an indirect/forecast source. The IMF analysis is scenario-based and does not predict specific outcomes for India; it frames the risk of uneven AI dividends.
