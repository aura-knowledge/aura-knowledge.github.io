# Summary

Robert Evenson and Douglas Gollin evaluate the diffusion and effects of high-yielding variety (HYV) seeds across developing countries. They find that HYVs raised yields significantly, particularly for wheat and rice, and helped prevent food-shortfall losses. The benefits were strongest in regions with reliable irrigation and supportive policy environments. The paper also notes distributional and environmental trade-offs, including reduced crop diversity and regional concentration.

# Key points for this article

- HYV adoption increased cereal production and averted famine risk in India and elsewhere.
- Gains were concentrated in wheat and rice and in well-irrigated regions.
- Environmental and diversity costs emerged as secondary effects.

# Limits

- The paper is an economic assessment, not an ecological or nutritional study.
- Regional variation within India is summarized at a broad level.
