# Summary

FAO AQUASTAT tracks water withdrawal, irrigation, and renewable water-resource pressure globally. For South Asia, including the Indo-Gangetic plain, the data show that agricultural groundwater extraction often exceeds recharge, leading to falling water tables and, in some areas, soil salinity and waterlogging. The costs are cumulative and diffuse rather than sudden.

# Key points for this article

- Intensive irrigation for high-yield cereals has stressed groundwater in India's grain belt.
- Water depletion is a delayed, regionally concentrated cost of the Green Revolution package.
- The data support the claim that ecological costs were politically harder to address than the initial hunger crisis.

# Limits

- AQUASTAT provides aggregate indicators; local impacts vary by management and geology.
- It does not attribute causation solely to the Green Revolution.
