## Summary

Elizabeth Eisenstein's *The Printing Press as an Agent of Change* (1979) is a foundational two-volume study of how printing reshaped early modern Europe. She argues that print culture enabled the standardization, preservation, and cross-referencing of texts, which underpinned the scientific revolution and the Reformation. At the same time, the new medium lowered the cost of producing and circulating polemical, prophetic, and unreliable material long before editorial and legal institutions developed.
