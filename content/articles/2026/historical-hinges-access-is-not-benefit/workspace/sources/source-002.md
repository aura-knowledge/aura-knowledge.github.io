## Summary

Erik Brynjolfsson, Daniel Rock, and Chad Syverson's NBER working paper "The Productivity J-Curve" explains why information and communication technologies often show a lag between adoption and measured productivity growth. Firms must typically redesign workflows, retrain workers, and accumulate intangible organizational capital before the productivity benefits of new technologies become visible in aggregate statistics. The paper uses historical and contemporary examples to argue that the paradox is a measurement and implementation lag, not a failure of the technology itself.
