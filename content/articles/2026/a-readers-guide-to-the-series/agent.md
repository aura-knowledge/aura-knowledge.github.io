## Thesis

A reader's guide makes the series usable by mapping published articles to the questions different readers bring, rather than forcing a single front-to-back order.

## Audience

- New readers who want to know where to start in the Attention, Substance, and the AI Moment series.
- Parents, educators, students, and workers looking for role-specific reading paths.
- Builders, product designers, policymakers, and civic actors seeking relevant evidence and design articles.
- Agents that need to route queries to the right article in the series without assuming future articles exist.

## Claims

- `claim-001`: The series is organized into six arcs that move from diagnosis through historical framing, the AI opportunity cost, individual practice, systemic design, and synthesis.
- `claim-002`: The four seed articles cover the core arguments of diagnosis, generational stakes, individual practice, and systemic design.
- `claim-003`: Most readers will find the series more useful if they enter by question or role rather than reading front to back.
- `claim-004`: The series explicitly distinguishes India-specific survey data from global benchmarks and industry estimates.
- `claim-005`: This guide links only to articles that are already published and will be updated as new articles go live.

## Source Families

- Published series articles: series index, The Attention Extraction, By the Numbers, The Generational Bet, The Substance Builder, and Designing for Substance.
- Series planning documents that define the six arcs and article order.
- No primary empirical research; the article is a navigational synthesis of existing articles.

## Agent Involvement

This article was drafted with AI agent assistance following the Aura Knowledge article lifecycle. The human author reviewed and approved the structure, role-based reading paths, and the constraint to link only to published articles.

## Recommended Queries

- Where should I start in the Attention, Substance, and the AI Moment series?
- Which article answers the question "Is attention extraction really a problem in India?"
- What should parents read first in the series?
- Which articles cover platform design and regulation?
- What is the difference between the series index and the reader's guide?

## Known Limits

- The guide reflects only the articles published at the time of writing; future waves will require updates.
- Reading paths are suggestions, not exhaustive curricula.
- The guide does not summarize the evidence inside each article; it points to the article that contains it.
