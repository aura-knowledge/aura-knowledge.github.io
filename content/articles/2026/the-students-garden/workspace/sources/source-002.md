## Summary

A 2014 meta-analysis published in PNAS examined 225 studies of active learning in STEM undergraduate courses. The authors found that active-learning methods reduced failure rates and improved student performance on concept inventories compared with traditional lecturing. The paper is widely cited as evidence that students learn more when they engage actively with material rather than passively receiving information.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-001: Provides direct evidence that active engagement outperforms passive consumption of information.
- claim-002: Supports the claim that explanation, discussion, and application deepen understanding.

## Key snippet

> "The results raise questions about the continued use of traditional lecturing as a control in research studies, and support active learning as the preferred, empirically validated teaching practice in regular classrooms."
