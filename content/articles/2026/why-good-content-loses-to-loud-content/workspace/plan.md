# Research Plan: why-good-content-loses-to-loud-content

## Question

Why does quality content often lose reach to louder, lower-quality content on algorithmic platforms, and what can quality creators do about it?

## Scope

In scope:
- Platform recommendation incentives
- Emotional/controversial content dynamics
- Reach concentration and audience head starts
- India-specific creator-economy pressures
- Practical strategies for quality creators

Out of scope:
- Platform-specific growth tactics
- Specific creator case studies
- Regulatory policy recommendations

## Expected claims

- claim-001: Recommendation systems optimize for predicted engagement rather than quality.
- claim-002: Emotional/surprising content spreads faster than neutral content.
- claim-003: Large creators get a distribution head start.
- claim-004: Quality creators face structural disadvantage without engagement mastery.
- claim-005: India's low payouts and AI deepen the pressure.
- claim-006: Quality creators can build resilience through non-feed reputation and trust-based models.

## Source strategy

- Peer-reviewed studies on algorithmic amplification (Milli et al., Vosoughi et al.)
- Psychology/sociology of online outrage (Yale study)
- Industry reports on creator economics (BCG, CreatorIQ)
- India-specific ad-rate estimates

## Risks and open questions

- Causal claims about algorithmic effects are mostly observational.
- India-specific algorithmic studies are scarce; global findings are applied structurally.
- Platform algorithms change frequently.
