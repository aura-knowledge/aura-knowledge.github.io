## Summary

The Digital Services Act (DSA) establishes transparency, accountability, and due-process obligations for online platforms operating in the European Union. Large platforms face additional requirements on algorithmic accountability, risk assessments, and advertising transparency. The DSA is one contemporary attempt to regulate platform power without banning services outright.

## Relevance to this article

Provides a real-world reference for claim-003: the right level of regulation is contested, and the DSA illustrates one calibration between user protection and platform operation. Also supports claim-004 by showing that the attention economy is shaped by policy choices rather than being technologically determined.

## Limitations

The DSA is a European framework and may not map directly onto India's constitutional, federal, and developmental context. Its effects on user behavior, platform design, and market structure are still unfolding as enforcement begins.
