# Source Note: Orben & Przybylski (2019)

**id:** source-oxford-mental-health-causality  
**title:** Orben & Przybylski, Nature Human Behaviour: The association between adolescent well-being and digital technology use  
**url:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-019-0604-1  
**type:** peer-reviewed-study  
**accessed:** 2026-07-05

## Summary

A large-scale meta-analysis using time-use diaries and self-report data from adolescents finds that the association between digital technology use and well-being is small, inconsistent, and sensitive to model specification. The authors argue that most existing research is correlational and cannot support strong causal claims about technology causing harm.

## Relevance to this article

Supports the open-question framing of claim-001: even where correlations exist, isolating the causal effect of specific platform features on mental health remains methodologically difficult. Also relevant to claim-002 because effect sizes vary across populations and measurement approaches.

## Limitations

The study is not India-specific. It focuses on adolescents and well-being rather than productivity, learning, or civic engagement. It does not rule out causal effects for specific subgroups or specific design features.
