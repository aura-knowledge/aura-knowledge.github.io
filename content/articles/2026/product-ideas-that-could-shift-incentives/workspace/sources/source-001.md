## Summary

Stack Exchange is a network of question-and-answer communities in which content is ranked by community voting, accepted answers, and a reputation score that accumulates across a user’s contributions. Reputation unlocks privileges, creating an incentive for sustained, helpful participation rather than one-off viral posts.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-001: Voting and accepted answers act as quality signals that complement raw engagement.
- claim-002: Community moderation shows how ranking can be aligned with collective usefulness.
- claim-003: Reputation accumulates over time, rewarding consistency rather than virality.

## Key snippet

> Users gain or lose reputation based on the quality of their questions and answers as judged by the community, with higher reputation unlocking additional moderation tools.
