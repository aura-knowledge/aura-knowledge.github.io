## Summary

Mozilla’s YouTube Regrets project crowdsourced reports of videos that people regretted watching after being recommended by YouTube’s algorithm. The research found that engagement-driven recommendations can push users toward harmful, misleading, or disturbing content, highlighting the public consequences of ranking objectives optimized solely for watch time.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-002: Provides evidence that engagement-only algorithms can surface harmful content, motivating public-interest alternatives.
- claim-004: Illustrates that users often lack meaningful control over algorithmic recommendations.

## Key snippet

> Volunteers reported videos recommended by YouTube that they later regretted watching, including misinformation, violent content, and hate speech, suggesting that the recommendation system can amplify harm.
