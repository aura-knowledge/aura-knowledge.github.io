## Summary

The WHO report surveys the global tobacco epidemic, documenting the historical framing of tobacco as a personal or socially acceptable choice and the gradual shift toward evidence-based regulation. It covers marketing practices, health impacts, and the Framework Convention on Tobacco Control as a design-level intervention.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-001: Supports the historical marketing frame and the epidemiological shift.
- claim-003: Supports the multi-factor transition (evidence, advocacy, regulation).
- claim-004: Provides an analogy for the long arc from personal choice to design accountability.

## Key snippet

> "Tobacco use is one of the biggest public health threats the world has ever faced... Most tobacco users begin as children, and the tobacco industry continues to target young people." — WHO tobacco fact sheets and reports frame the issue as a population-level design problem, not only individual choice.
