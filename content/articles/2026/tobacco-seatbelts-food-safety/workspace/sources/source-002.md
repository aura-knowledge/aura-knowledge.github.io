## Summary

NHTSA publishes national seatbelt-use statistics and describes the combination of state laws, enforcement, reminder systems, and vehicle safety standards that increased seatbelt use over time. The data show that structural defaults changed behavior more effectively than information campaigns alone.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-002: Supports the argument that seatbelt rules worked through design defaults and standards.
- claim-003: Illustrates the role of regulation and enforcement in a public-health transition.
- claim-004: Provides an analogy for how design defaults can reshape population-level behavior.

## Key snippet

> "Seat belts are the single most effective way to reduce deaths and injuries in motor vehicle crashes." — NHTSA emphasizes that the protection comes from built-in vehicle design and use laws, not from driver discretion alone.
