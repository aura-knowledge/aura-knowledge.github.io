# source-001: Ericsson on deliberate practice

## Citation

Ericsson, K. A., Krampe, R. T., & Tesch-Römer, C. (1993). The role of deliberate practice in the acquisition of expert performance. *Psychological Review*, 100(3), 363–406. https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1993-40718-001

## Core idea

Expert performance is acquired primarily through deliberate practice—repeated attempts at tasks just beyond current ability, with immediate feedback, correction, and progressively harder goals. Innate talent plays a smaller role than is commonly assumed.

## Relevance to the article

Supports the claim that early work is expected to be imperfect and that the gap between taste and ability closes through volume of focused, feedback-rich practice.

## Limits

The original studies focused on domains like music and chess; transfer to open-ended creative or knowledge-work skills is plausible but less tightly demonstrated.
