## Thesis

In India, engagement-based ranking and weak accountability mechanisms systematically amplify outrage and divisive content, eroding the social trust that public problem-solving requires.

## Audience

- Indian citizens, students, and educators trying to understand why public discourse online feels increasingly polarized.
- Policymakers and regulators designing platform accountability and duty-of-care frameworks.
- Builders and product designers interested in ranking objectives, recommendation systems, and alternative engagement metrics.
- Researchers studying platform behavior, misinformation, and social cohesion in mobile-first democracies.

## Claims

- `claim-001`: On major social platforms, engagement-based ranking systematically amplifies emotionally charged and divisive content because anger and moral outrage generate more likes, shares, and comments than neutral or constructive material.
- `claim-002`: Users who receive more likes and shares for expressing moral outrage tend to express more outrage over time, especially in politically moderate networks.
- `claim-003`: On Twitter/X, engagement-based feeds contained a higher share of angry political tweets than chronological feeds in a large-scale audit.
- `claim-004`: Indian influencers on Twitter received higher retweet rates for polarizing-event tweets than for their other tweets in a 2022 Microsoft Research Bengaluru study.
- `claim-005`: The World Economic Forum's Global Risks Report 2024 ranked India highest among surveyed countries for misinformation and disinformation risk over the next two years.
- `claim-006`: India Hate Lab documented a 74.4% increase in hate-speech events targeting religious minorities in 2024, with digital platforms playing a significant amplification role.
- `claim-007`: In a sampled set of viral WhatsApp groups in India, only one in 158 instances of misinformation was actively corrected inside the group chat.
- `claim-008`: Central and South Asia accounted for only 5% of cases selected by the Meta Oversight Board in 2023, despite India being Meta's largest user market.

## Known Limits

- Most platform-behavior studies are based on Twitter/X data and may not generalize directly to Meta, YouTube, or WhatsApp in India.
- The WhatsApp correction finding comes from a limited, consent-based sample of private groups.
- The Meta Oversight Board case-share figure is a proxy for accountability attention, not a direct measure of safety investment or staffing.
- Causal claims are avoided where underlying evidence is correlational or observational.
