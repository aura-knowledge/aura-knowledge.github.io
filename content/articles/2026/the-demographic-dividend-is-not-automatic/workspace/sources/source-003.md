## Summary

NASSCOM publications on India's technology talent landscape describe a demand-supply gap for digital skills, including data science, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and cloud computing. The gap is framed as both a challenge for employers and an opportunity for skill-building at scale.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-002: Indirectly supports the employability gap by describing employer demand for skills that graduates often lack.
- claim-003: Provides industry context for youth unemployment among technically educated cohorts.
- claim-004: Supports the time-use-dividend argument by showing that how young people allocate learning time affects national competitiveness.

## Key snippet

> India's technology sector faces a widening talent gap in emerging digital skills, even as graduate numbers rise, pointing to a mismatch between education output and industry demand.
