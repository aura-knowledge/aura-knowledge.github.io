## Thesis

Vulnerable users—children, elderly people, and low-literacy users—deserve stronger default protections, age-appropriate design, and limits on data-driven persuasion because their attention and data are not fair targets for extraction.

## Key Claims

- `claim-001`: Vulnerable users deserve stronger default protections because their attention and data are not fair targets for extraction.
- `claim-002`: Children's developing brains are more susceptible to variable rewards and social comparison.
- `claim-003`: Elderly users face higher risks from scams, misinformation, and platform complexity.
- `claim-004`: Low-literacy users may not recognize dark patterns or understand data practices.
- `claim-005`: Age-appropriate design standards, such as the UK's Age Appropriate Design Code, offer a regulatory template.
