## Summary

Nir Eyal's *Hooked* presents a four-step model—trigger, action, variable reward, investment—for designing products that form habits. The variable-reward phase and external triggers are especially relevant to feed and notification design, though Eyal frames the model as a tool for any product designer rather than only for extraction.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-001: Describes variable rewards as the phase that sustains repeated engagement.
- claim-002: Discusses reducing friction to make the next action as easy as possible.
- claim-003: Covers external triggers such as notifications that pull users back into the habit loop.

## Key snippet

> "Variable rewards are the third step in the Hook Model, and there are three types of variable rewards: the tribe, the hunt, and the self." (Chapter on variable reward)
