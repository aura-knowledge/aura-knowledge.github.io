## Summary

The Open Government Partnership's civic-tech guidance surveys how technology can support transparency, accountability, and citizen participation. It emphasizes that effective civic technology is sustained by relationships among citizens, civil society, and government, and that one-off apps rarely succeed without local trust networks and institutional follow-through.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-001: Supports the framing that civic information often fails to reach citizens due to fragmentation and lack of accessible channels.
- claim-003: Reinforces the argument that local intermediaries and sustained relationships are essential for civic tech to work.
- claim-004: Provides grounding for the idea that documented, shared civic knowledge can become community infrastructure.

## Key snippet

> Civic tech is not just about building apps; it is about strengthening the relationship between governments and citizens.
