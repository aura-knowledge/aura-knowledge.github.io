## Summary

A collection of internal Facebook documents leaked to regulators and journalists in 2021, disclosing internal research on topics including teen mental health, algorithmic amplification, and content moderation gaps. The disclosures fueled congressional hearings in the United States and regulatory attention globally. This Wikipedia article is a secondary aggregator and pointer to primary reporting.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-002: Provides the central example of how whistleblower leaks can force public debate and regulatory scrutiny.
- claim-001: Internal research in the papers often echoed or amplified external academic findings on harms.
- claim-003: Employee dissent and the disclosure of internal risk analyses increased business and regulatory pressure on the company.

## Key snippet

> The Facebook Papers are a set of internal documents created by Facebook that were leaked to the Securities and Exchange Commission and provided to Congress in redacted form by Facebook whistleblower Frances Haugen's legal counsel.
