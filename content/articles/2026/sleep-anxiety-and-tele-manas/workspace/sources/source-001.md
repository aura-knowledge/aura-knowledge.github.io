# Source note: Tele-MANAS PIB release

**Source ID:** source-tele-manas-pib  
**URL:** https://pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2045678  
**Type:** Government press release  
**Accessed:** 2026-07-05

## Key points

- Tele-MANAS, India's national mental-health helpline, had completed more than 32 lakh calls.
- Approximately 70% of calls were from people in the 18–45 age group.
- The helpline provides free, anonymous counseling and is a rare source of population-facing mental-health demand data.

## How the article uses it

The call volume is used as a public-health signal of unmet distress, especially among young adults. The article does not treat callers as a representative sample or assume that all calls reflect clinical disorders.

## Limits

- Callers are self-selected; the data is not a nationally representative survey.
- The release does not break down call reasons in detail, so the share attributable to anxiety, sleep, or screen-related stress is not directly measured.
