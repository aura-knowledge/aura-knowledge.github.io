# Source note: NCERT school mental-health survey

**Source ID:** source-ncert-mental-health-2022  
**URL:** https://ncert.nic.in/pdf/announcement/Mental_Health_Survey.pdf  
**Type:** Government report  
**Accessed:** 2026-07-05

## Key points

- Survey covered more than 3.5 lakh school students across India.
- Found high prevalence of anxiety, mood disturbance, and sleep problems.
- Results have been echoed in several state-level student mental-health surveys.

## How the article uses it

The NCERT data is treated as a pre-clinical barometer: it captures adolescent distress before it reaches the threshold of a helpline call or clinical diagnosis. It supports the argument that the signal is broad and not limited to urban or affluent students.

## Limits

- Responses are self-reported and may be influenced by social desirability or by how questions were framed.
- The survey does not measure screen time or establish causal links to digital habits; those connections rely on separate peer-reviewed research.
