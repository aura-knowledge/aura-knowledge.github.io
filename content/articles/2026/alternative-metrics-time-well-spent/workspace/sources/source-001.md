## Summary

The Center for Humane Technology is a nonprofit organization that documents how attention-economy business models and product-design patterns—autoplay, infinite scroll, variable rewards, notification badges—shape user behavior. Its public materials frame the problem as a structural mismatch between platform optimization targets and human flourishing.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-001: Provides background that engagement metrics encode a business model and shape design choices.
- claim-002: Supplies the broader argument that alternative metrics should include well-being, meaningful connection, learning, and civic engagement.
- claim-004: Supports the view that making optimization targets visible and debatable is a prerequisite for reform.
