## Summary

Paul Klemperer's review article surveys how switching costs affect market competition. Consumers who would lose existing relationships, learned skills, or accumulated data when changing suppliers are less likely to switch even when better alternatives exist. This softens price and quality competition and can entrench incumbent firms.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-002: Provides economic grounding for the claim that switching costs include lost contacts, content archives, and reputation.
- claim-003: Supports the argument that reducing switching costs—through portability and interoperability—can restore competition and expand user choice.

## Key snippet

> Switching costs make markets less competitive, because firms know that consumers who buy from them today are less likely to switch to a rival tomorrow.
