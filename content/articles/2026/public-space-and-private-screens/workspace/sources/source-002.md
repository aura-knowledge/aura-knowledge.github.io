## Summary

A 2015 Pew Research Center survey on mobile etiquette found that 23% of cellphone owners said they used their phones in public spaces specifically to avoid interacting with others. The report documents how smartphones reshape norms of public behavior, attention, and social withdrawal in shared spaces.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-002: Offers an international benchmark for how public spaces become sites of individual screen use.
- claim-004: Supports the argument that device use can convert shared space into parallel solitude by making withdrawal feel normal and acceptable.

## Key snippet

> 23% of all cellphone owners say that when they are in public spaces they use their phone to avoid interacting with others who are near them.
