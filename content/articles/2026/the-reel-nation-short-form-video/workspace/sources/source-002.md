**type:** peer-reviewed-study
**accessed:** 2026-07-05

## Summary

A peer-reviewed study in BMC Psychiatry surveyed Chinese college students and found associations between short-form video addiction and symptoms of anxiety, depression, and sleep disturbance. The study uses self-report scales and frames its findings as correlational.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-003: supports the link between heavy short-form video use and mental-health correlates, including anxiety and sleep disruption.

## Key snippet

> "Short-form video addiction was positively associated with anxiety, depression, and sleep problems among college students, although the cross-sectional design limits causal inference."

## Limitations

The sample is Chinese college students, so direct generalization to Indian populations requires caution.
