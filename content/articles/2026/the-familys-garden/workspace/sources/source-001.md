## Summary

A clinical report from the American Academy of Pediatrics reviewing the effects of media on children from birth through early school age. It emphasizes that parent media use is a strong predictor of child media habits, that background media can disrupt play and parent-child interaction, and that co-viewing and family media plans are more effective than unilateral restrictions.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-001: Parent screen use shapes child screen use from infancy.
- claim-003: Age-appropriate, consistent limits are recommended over blanket prohibitions.

## Key snippet

> "Parent media use is the strongest predictor of child media use... Parents should be encouraged to develop a family media use plan."
