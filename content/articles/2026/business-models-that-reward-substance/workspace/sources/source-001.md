## Summary

Aggregation Theory explains how modern internet platforms gain power by controlling demand (users) rather than supply (content or goods). They commoditize suppliers, centralize user relationships, and monetize through advertising or transaction fees. The theory implies that ad-funded platforms have a structural incentive to maximize attention because attention is the scarce resource they sell to advertisers.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-001: Provides the structural argument that ad-funded platforms optimize for attention/engagement because their business model rewards captured demand.

## Key snippet

> "The value chain for any given consumer market is divided into three parts: suppliers, distributors, and consumers/users. The best way to make outsize profits in any of these markets is to either gain a horizontal monopoly in one differing part of the value chain, or to integrate two of the parts such that you have a competitive advantage in delivering a vertical solution."
