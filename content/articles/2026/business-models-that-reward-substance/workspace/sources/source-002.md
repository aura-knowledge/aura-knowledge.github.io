## Summary

The Reuters Institute Digital News Report is an annual survey of news consumption and funding across multiple countries. It documents the continued dominance of free, ad-supported news alongside slow but steady growth in subscriptions, memberships, and donations. In many mobile-first markets, including parts of Asia, alternative funding models remain small but are expanding as creators and publishers experiment with direct reader revenue.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-002: Provides comparative context on how subscriptions and memberships are growing as alternatives to advertising-funded news.

## Key snippet

> "While most people still prefer to access news for free, a growing minority in richer countries are paying for online news, often through subscriptions, memberships, or donations. In many lower-income markets, direct payment models remain nascent but are increasingly part of the conversation about sustainability."
