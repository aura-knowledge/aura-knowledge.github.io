## Summary

BJ Fogg's *Tiny Habits* proposes a behavior model in which motivation, ability, and a prompt must align for a behavior to occur. Fogg argues that making a behavior tiny—so small it requires almost no motivation—makes it far more likely to stick than relying on willpower. The approach pairs a new tiny behavior with an existing anchor (e.g., "after I pour morning coffee, I will open my learning app") and celebrates success to wire in the habit.

## Relevance to claims

- claim-002: Directly supports the argument that small, consistent actions compound more reliably than large, heroic efforts.
- claim-001: Provides a method for reclaiming short windows such as commutes and waiting time without depending on peak motivation.

## Key snippet

> "Take a behavior you want, make it tiny, find where it fits naturally in your life, and nurture its growth."
